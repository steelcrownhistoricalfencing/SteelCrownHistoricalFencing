    Safety Standards

    Purpose

    The purpose of these standards is to maintain a safe, controlled, and professional training environment for all participants at Steel Crown Historical Fencing.

    Historical European Martial Arts involve inherent physical risk. While risk cannot be completely eliminated, it can and must be managed responsibly through proper equipment, control, supervision, and conduct.

    Safety and intensity are not mutually exclusive. Safety enables intensity.

    Every participant shares responsibility for maintaining a safe training environment.

    1. General Safety Expectations

    All students, instructors, and participants are expected to:

    • Follow instructor direction during drills and sparring
    • Maintain control of themselves and their weapon at all times
    • Train within the intensity level and rules established for the activity
    • Stop immediately when a halt is called
    • Report unsafe conditions, damaged equipment, or injuries
    • Conduct themselves responsibly during all club activities

    Reckless behavior, uncontrolled strikes, or intentional unsafe conduct will not be tolerated.

    2. Equipment Standards

    Participants are responsible for ensuring that their equipment is safe, functional, and appropriate for the level of training being performed.

    Protective equipment requirements may vary depending on the activity, including:

    • Technical drills
    • Controlled sparring
    • Steel sparring
    • Tournament preparation
    • Competitive events

    Required equipment may include:

    • Approved fencing mask
    • Throat protection
    • Hand protection
    • Padded jacket
    • Forearm and elbow protection
    • Groin or chest protection
    • Knee protection
    • Other equipment as determined by instructors or club leadership

    Leadership reserves the right to inspect, restrict, or prohibit any equipment deemed unsafe or inappropriate for training.

    3. Calibration and Control

    Control and appropriate calibration are fundamental expectations of all participants.

    Training intensity should match:

    • The experience of the participants
    • The purpose of the exercise
    • The direction of the instructor

    Participants are expected to fence responsibly and maintain awareness of their training partner's safety and skill level.

    More experienced students are expected to demonstrate greater control and adjust intensity appropriately when training with less experienced participants.

    Competitive intensity is encouraged when appropriate. Recklessness is not.

    4. Sparring Standards

    All sparring activities must be conducted under established club rules and instructor supervision.

    Participants are expected to:

    • Maintain control during exchanges
    • Acknowledge valid hits honestly
    • Respect halt commands immediately
    • Avoid unnecessary escalation
    • Prioritize safety over winning exchanges

    Additional sparring restrictions or requirements may be established depending on the weapon, intensity, or training format being used.

    Unauthorized sparring or unsafe conduct may result in restricted participation.

    5. Injury and Medical Response

    All injuries must be reported to an instructor or club leadership immediately.

    Participants experiencing symptoms of concussion, dizziness, loss of awareness, or other serious injury will be removed from training activity until it is deemed safe to return.

    Club leadership reserves the right to restrict participation following injury if continued training may present a safety risk to the individual or others.

    Participants are encouraged to train responsibly and communicate openly regarding injuries or medical concerns that may affect safe participation.

    6. Authority and Enforcement

    Club leadership and instructors retain the authority to stop any activity or restrict participation if unsafe conditions or conduct are observed.

    Participation may be limited or revoked due to:

    • Unsafe behavior
    • Failure to follow safety instructions
    • Inadequate protective equipment
    • Repeated reckless conduct
    • Behavior that jeopardizes the safety of others

    The safety of participants and the long-term health of the training environment take priority over individual participation.
