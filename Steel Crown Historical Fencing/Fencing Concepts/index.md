# Fencing Concepts

[← Back to Materials](../Materials/index.md){ .md-button }

Techniques are specific. Principles are general.

This section covers foundational concepts that apply across weapon systems and historical traditions. Understanding these principles gives you a framework for reading exchanges, diagnosing why something failed, and making better decisions under pressure, regardless of what you are holding.

The concepts here are not alternatives to the weapon-specific curricula. They are the underlying logic that makes those curricula coherent. A student who understands lines, openings, opposition, and structure will learn specific techniques faster and retain them better.

---

## What's Inside

**The Four Openings** — How the body is divided into targets, and why that vocabulary matters for every exchange.

**Inside and Outside Line** — The most practically useful spatial concept in fencing. Where your weapon is relative to your opponent's determines what options are available to both of you.

**Opposition and Cover** — The difference between deflecting an attack and controlling the line. Why some defenses hold and others don't.

**Structure** — What makes a position strong. The difference between a blade that looks threatening and one that actually is.
