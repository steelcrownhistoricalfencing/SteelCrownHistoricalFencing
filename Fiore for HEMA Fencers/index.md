# Fiore dei Liberi for the Modern Fencer

*A structured guide to the longsword system of Fior di Battaglia (c. 1409)*

This site presents the martial art of **Fiore dei Liberi** — the 15th-century Italian master whose *Fior di Battaglia* (Flower of Battle) remains one of the most complete and systematically organized fencing manuals to survive from the medieval period.

The material here is organized as a curriculum for the modern practitioner, moving from historical context through the guards (poste), the blows (colpi), and into tactical application. Everything is grounded in the Getty manuscript, the earliest and most authoritative version of the text.

---

## How to Use This Guide

This is not a book to read once from front to back. It is a reference to return to as your understanding deepens. Each section builds on the others, but you can enter from any point.

**New to Fiore?** Start with the History section to understand who Fiore was and how his system is organized. Then read the Glossary to get the terminology grounded before working with the guards.

**Working on a specific guard?** Go directly to the Guards section. Each guard file covers the original manuscript text, physical structure, tactical function, bilateral variations, and training drills.

**Drilling strikes?** The Strikes section covers all seven blows individually — Fendente, Mezzano, and Sottano each get separate Destra and Sinestra files, with the Punta treated as its own chapter.
